Customer Asks for Refund — How Wiom Decides

Updated: 2026-04-02

Context: Customer has churned and is calling Wiom asking for their security deposit refund. Wiom's decision to process the refund is based on confirmed signals about where the device is. The CS agent's job is to identify the strongest available signal — and from that, make a call: refund, wait, or dispute.

Four actors must do their job for a refund to complete:
1. Customer's job — Hand over the router + Submit UPI
2. Partner's job — Collect the router + Close the PUT successfully
3. 3PL's job — Collect router from customer (when partner fails) + Send back to Wiom warehouse
4. Wiom's job — Evaluate device location signal → make refund decision → process via Wiom Hub

2. No-UPI Flow — Two Streams

Context: Wiom has confirmed device location and decided to refund — but the customer has not submitted a UPI. Two streams: Stream 1 is system-triggered while the customer is still active. Stream 2 (Wiom Hub) only kicks in after the customer is archived and calls CS.

Key rule: After PUT closes as Router_recovered, customer still has 28 days of app access (chat, UPI submission, etc). At day 28, customer is archived and loses app access. By the time Stream 2 is triggered, archival has already happened — no need to check again.

Open question: For PUT expired + device returned to warehouse by 3PL/RA — how many days does the customer remain active on app before archival? Need to check.

Manual Refund — Wiom Hub (Ops Portal)

Updated: 2026-04-01

Premise: Device has been located and the refund decision has been made. This flow is not about identifying the signal — it is about what happens after. Ops lead uses Wiom Hub to process the refund manually.

Entry point: Wiom Hub → Users tab
  — Active customer: Customers → search by mobile → open record → Netbox Security Refund sub-tab
  — Archived customer: Archived Customers → search by mobile → open record → Netbox Security Refund sub-tab

Access: Restricted to high-level members only (role-gated in Wiom Hub).
Daily global limits: Capped by # of transactions AND total ₹ amount — whichever is hit first blocks further actions for the day. Alert sent to ops manager when limit is reached.
Open item — Refund amount: The exact amount to be refunded per customer is to be confirmed. Jaivin to take inputs from Bhavin before this goes to tech.

Updated: 2026-04-07

Premise: Two scenarios where Wiom has the strongest possible confirmation that a device has been recovered — no CS action is needed. System detects the condition and initiates the Juspay payout directly.

Key rules: No daily cap applies (strongest-signal scenarios). Per-customer cap of 1 still applies (system + manual combined). Conflict guard: if CS has already manually initiated a refund for this user_account_id, auto-trigger does not fire. G0 eligibility (Bhavin's segment rules) still applies.
